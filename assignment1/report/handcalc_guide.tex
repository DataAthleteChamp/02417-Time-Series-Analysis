\section*{Section 2.1 --- Matrix Form (write this on paper)}

The linear trend model:
\[
Y_t = \theta_1 + \theta_2 \, x_t + \varepsilon_t, \qquad \varepsilon_t \sim N(0, \sigma^2), \quad t = 1, \ldots, N.
\]

In matrix form for the first 3 time points:
\[
\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} Y_1 \\ Y_2 \\ Y_3 \end{bmatrix}}_{\boldsymbol{y}}
=
\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 1 & x_1 \\ 1 & x_2 \\ 1 & x_3 \end{bmatrix}}_{\boldsymbol{X}}
\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} \theta_1 \\ \theta_2 \end{bmatrix}}_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}
+
\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} \varepsilon_1 \\ \varepsilon_2 \\ \varepsilon_3 \end{bmatrix}}_{\boldsymbol{\varepsilon}}
\]

Inserting the actual values (3 decimal places):
\[
\boldsymbol{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2.930 \\ 2.934 \\ 2.941 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad
\boldsymbol{X} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2018.000 \\ 1 & 2018.083 \\ 1 & 2018.167 \end{bmatrix}
\]

So the full equation with numbers is:
\[
\begin{bmatrix} 2.930 \\ 2.934 \\ 2.941 \end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2018.000 \\ 1 & 2018.083 \\ 1 & 2018.167 \end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix} \theta_1 \\ \theta_2 \end{bmatrix}
+
\begin{bmatrix} \varepsilon_1 \\ \varepsilon_2 \\ \varepsilon_3 \end{bmatrix}
\]

\textbf{That's it for 2.1.} Write the general form, then plug in the numbers.

\section*{Section 5.1 --- RLS Hand Calculation (write this on paper)}

\subsection*{Step 0: Write the update equations}

The RLS algorithm updates at each time step $t$:
\begin{align}
\boldsymbol{R}_t &= \boldsymbol{R}_{t-1} + \boldsymbol{x}_t \, \boldsymbol{x}_t^\top \label{eq:Rupdate}\\[6pt]
\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_t &= \hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_{t-1} + \boldsymbol{R}_t^{-1} \, \boldsymbol{x}_t \bigl( Y_t - \boldsymbol{x}_t^\top \hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_{t-1} \bigr) \label{eq:thetaupdate}
\end{align}

Initialize with:
\[
\boldsymbol{R}_0 = \begin{bmatrix} 0.1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.1 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad
\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_0 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}
\]

%% ----------------------------------------
\subsection*{Iteration $t=1$}
%% ----------------------------------------

\textbf{Given:}
\[
\boldsymbol{x}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2018 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad y_1 = 2.930
\]

\textbf{Step 1 --- Compute $\boldsymbol{x}_1 \boldsymbol{x}_1^\top$:}
\[
\boldsymbol{x}_1 \boldsymbol{x}_1^\top
= \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2018 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2018 \end{bmatrix}
= \begin{bmatrix} 1 \times 1 & 1 \times 2018 \\ 2018 \times 1 & 2018 \times 2018 \end{bmatrix}
= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2018 \\ 2018 & 4\,072\,324 \end{bmatrix}
\]

\textbf{Step 2 --- Compute $\boldsymbol{R}_1$:}
\[
\boldsymbol{R}_1 = \boldsymbol{R}_0 + \boldsymbol{x}_1 \boldsymbol{x}_1^\top
= \begin{bmatrix} 0.1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.1 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2018 \\ 2018 & 4\,072\,324 \end{bmatrix}
= \begin{bmatrix} 1.10 & 2018.00 \\ 2018.00 & 4\,072\,324.10 \end{bmatrix}
\]

\textbf{Step 3 --- Compute innovation:}
\[
y_1 - \boldsymbol{x}_1^\top \hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_0
= 2.930 - \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2018 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}
= 2.930 - 0
= 2.930
\]

\textbf{Step 4 --- Compute $\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_1$:}
\[
\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_1
= \hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_0 + \boldsymbol{R}_1^{-1} \boldsymbol{x}_1 \cdot 2.930
= \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + \boldsymbol{R}_1^{-1} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2018 \end{bmatrix} \cdot 2.930
= \begin{bmatrix} 0.000001 \\ 0.001452 \end{bmatrix}
\approx \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0.00145 \end{bmatrix}
\]

\vspace{1em}

%% ----------------------------------------
\subsection*{Iteration $t=2$}
%% ----------------------------------------

\textbf{Given:}
\[
\boldsymbol{x}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2018.083 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad y_2 = 2.934
\]

\textbf{Step 1 --- Compute $\boldsymbol{x}_2 \boldsymbol{x}_2^\top$:}
\[
\boldsymbol{x}_2 \boldsymbol{x}_2^\top
= \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2018.083 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2018.083 \end{bmatrix}
= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2018.083 \\ 2018.083 & 4\,072\,660.34 \end{bmatrix}
\]

\textbf{Step 2 --- Compute $\boldsymbol{R}_2$:}
\[
\boldsymbol{R}_2 = \boldsymbol{R}_1 + \boldsymbol{x}_2 \boldsymbol{x}_2^\top
= \begin{bmatrix} 1.10 & 2018.00 \\ 2018.00 & 4\,072\,324.10 \end{bmatrix}
+ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2018.083 \\ 2018.083 & 4\,072\,660.34 \end{bmatrix}
\]
\[
= \begin{bmatrix} 2.10 & 4036.08 \\ 4036.08 & 8\,144\,984.44 \end{bmatrix}
\]

\textbf{Step 3 --- Compute innovation:}
\[
\boldsymbol{x}_2^\top \hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_1
= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2018.083 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0.000001 \\ 0.001452 \end{bmatrix}
= 1 \times 0.000001 + 2018.083 \times 0.001452
= 2.931
\]
\[
y_2 - \boldsymbol{x}_2^\top \hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_1 = 2.934 - 2.931 = 0.003
\]

\textbf{Step 4 --- Compute $\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_2$:}
\[
\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_2
= \hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}_1 + \boldsymbol{R}_2^{-1} \boldsymbol{x}_2 \cdot 0.003
= \begin{bmatrix} 0.000001 \\ 0.001452 \end{bmatrix} + \boldsymbol{R}_2^{-1} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2018.083 \end{bmatrix} \cdot 0.003
= \begin{bmatrix} 0.000000 \\ 0.001453 \end{bmatrix}
\approx \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0.00145 \end{bmatrix}
\]

\vspace{1em}
\hrule
\vspace{1em}

\textbf{Comment:} After just 2 observations the intercept $\hat\theta_1 \approx 0$ because with $x$-values around 2018, the slope $\hat\theta_2$ absorbs most of the signal. The estimates will stabilize as more data arrives. Note that we do not need to invert $\boldsymbol{R}_t$ by hand --- the key point is to show the \emph{structure} of the recursion.
